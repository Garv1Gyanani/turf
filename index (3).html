The Science of Suspension

Why Choose Suspension Practice?

Working with an aerial hammock allows you to experience deep alignments, restorative physical decompression, and supportive core development.

Improves Flexibility

Decompress joints and stretch tissues deeper with physical suspension support.

Builds Core

Engage your deep core muscles naturally as you find balance suspended above ground.

Relieves Stress

Gentle traction on the spine helps calm and soothe the nervous system.

Beginner Safe

Our classes are fully adjustable, making the practice approachable for beginners.

Supports Posture

Counteract daily sitting strain by expanding and lengthening tight vertebrae.

Backed by Research

Not Just a Trend — Tested by Science

Suspension practice has been studied by exercise physiologists and yoga researchers alike. Here is what published research says about aerial yoga's effect on the body.

320 Calories / 50-Min Class

An ACE-commissioned study found a single aerial session burns calories at a rate comparable to brisk walking or light cycling.

2–3mm Disc Height Gained

Spine research links supported inversion traction to a temporary increase in space between vertebrae, easing pressure on compressed nerves.

8 Weeks To Measurable Change

Yoga journal research recorded improved spinal flexibility and lower perceived stress in participants after eight weeks of regular practice.

6 Weeks Heart-Health Gains

A six-week aerial yoga intervention was shown to lower several cardiovascular risk markers, including resting heart rate and body-fat percentage.

The Progression Roadmap

Our Signature Method

We guide each student through three distinct structural phases to ensure a safe, satisfying transition to suspended aerial yoga.

Phase 01

Tethered Gravity Relief

We begin with the fabric adjusted close to the ground. This phase focuses on learning fabric holds, safe hand wraps, and letting the hammock support partial body weight to build comfort.

Level: Low-Suspension
Recommended Start
Phase 02

Spinal Decompression

Once comfortable, we move into gentle, low-altitude inversions. This allows the weight of the torso to traction the spine, helping ease pressure in the neck, shoulders, and lower back.

Level: Supported Inversions
Phase 03

Dynamic Suspension Flow

Students explore full suspension balances, transitions, and sequenced poses. This stage challenges and refines overall flexibility, core stability, and full-body alignment.

Level: Full Suspension Flow
